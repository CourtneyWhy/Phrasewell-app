Behavior Categories and Sub-Categories
The app has 8 main categories of behaviors, each with 7–10 sub-categories (specific behaviors). These are the tiles parents tap after "I need help right now." The categories cover the most common trauma-related challenges in foster/adoptive parenting. Below is the full list:

Food and eating
Hoarding food in room
Asking for food every 10 minutes
Gorging then hiding wrappers
Stealing food from pantry at night
Refusing to eat what is served
Throwing food when upset
Gorging at meals then vomiting
Hiding food under bed or in drawers

Aggression and safety risks
Hitting or kicking when angry
Throwing objects in rage
Biting when overwhelmed
Destroying property
Head banging on wall or floor
Hurting pets or siblings

Lying and dishonesty
Saying "I didn’t do it" when obvious
Rewriting history
Lying about homework or chores
Stealing praise or credit

Stealing and taking things
Taking sibling’s toy without asking
Stealing money or small items
Taking items from school or store
Hoarding small objects

Big feelings and meltdowns
Complete sobbing meltdown
Saying "I hate you" or "You are not my real mom"
Running away or hiding in house
Self-harm threats or head-banging
Shutdown and refusing to speak

Sensory and body struggles
Socks, seams, or tags meltdown
Refusing to brush teeth or hair
Food texture refusal or gagging
Loud noises or covering ears
Refusing baths or showers

Defiance and control
Saying "no" to every request
Deliberate slow-motion compliance
Arguing every direction
Running away from adult in public

Nighttime, sleep and bedtime
Getting out of bed repeatedly
Nightmares and waking screaming
Afraid of the dark
Bed-wetting accidents
Refusing to sleep alone


Selection Process
The app is designed for speed in a crisis — 3–4 taps to get help:

Parent opens app → taps "I need help right now" on home screen.
8 category tiles appear. Parent taps the one that matches the moment (e.g., Food and eating).
List of 7–10 behaviors in that category shows up. Parent taps the closest match (e.g., Hoarding food in room).
Detail screen opens with:
Exact script (what to say word-for-word, adapted to child's age range)
Regulation tool (quick calming action)
Backup tool (if first doesn't work)

If the script isn't working, tap "Give me a different one" → AI generates a fresh variation (unlimited, back-end query to AI model).
Tap "Log how this went" → pre-fills category, behavior, child (from My Kids), and takes to form for notes/mood.

Screen Layouts
The app has 5 simple screens (mobile-first, clean, sage green/cream colors, large buttons):

Home
Big title: "Calm Parent"
Subtitle: "Instant tools for hard moments"
Disclaimer: "Trauma-informed tools from a certified Stephen Minister & foster-to-adopt mom"
Main button: "I need help right now" (large, center)
Secondary buttons: "My Journal", "My Kids"

Categories
8 large tiles in 2-column grid (with emojis, tappable)
Back button to home

Behavior Detail
Behavior title at top
Large text block: Exact script
Bullet: Regulation tool
Bullet: Backup tool
Button: "Give me a different one" (AI refresh)
Button: "Log how this went" (bottom)

My Kids
List of added children (cards with name/age)
"+ Add Child" button → form: Name, Age dropdown (0-3, 4-7, etc.), Gender, Notes
Tap a child to select for logging

My Journal
List of logs (cards with date/child/category)
"+ New Log" button → form with pre-fills
Tap a log → detail with "Copy Email for Caseworker" and "Copy Text for Caseworker" buttons


Everything is private (local storage), no logins needed.
